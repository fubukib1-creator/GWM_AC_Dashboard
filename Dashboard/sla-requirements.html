False-confirm audit — same-day confirmation

Source: Install Service Management Platform §5 · 5% monthly fee deduction
5%
stage = InstallationCompleted  AND  workingDays( confirm_setup_date → delivery_date ) < 1
Confirmation and delivery happened on the same working day — the confirm step may have been a formality rather than real customer agreement. If systemic, triggers Platform §5 · 5% deduction of monthly fees. v3 routes these to leaf K4.B ("Suspicious confirm"); v2 raises the FALSE_CONFIRM_AUDIT flag.

Issue tracking — งานติดปัญหา

Source: Service Quality Assessment §2(b) and (f)
Cases where status.name === 'งานติดปัญหา' are surfaced as a categorical flag (orthogonal to §1–§3). The contract attaches QA penalties for unresolved customer issues and quality failures. Severity: medium baseline, escalates with age. v3 routes them to K*.B.* leaves based on the detail field root-cause.

Source clauses verbatim — Installation Service Response Time, pages 5–6

Response Time §1
Contact within 24 hours
"Party B shall contact the user within twenty-four (24) hours upon receipt of the installation service work order, confirm the installation order and user information, and make an appointment for on-site investigation as described in Attachment 2."
Response Time §2
Survey within 3 working days
"Party B shall conduct on-site investigation within three (3) working days upon receipt of the service work order. In case of customer inconvenience or other special reasons, Party B shall file with GWM for record, the timeline shall be extended accordingly and Party B shall not be liable for such delay."
Response Time §3
Install within 3 working days of approval
"[…] Party B shall work out an installation and construction plan for charging piles and complete the installation within three (3) working days upon approval of the users."
Escape valves not yet modelled in the dashboards. §2 (customer-inconvenience extension) and Installation Technical Specification §5 (force majeure: accidents, vehicle breakdown, adverse weather, etc.) both extend the SLA clock when filed for record. The dashboards currently have no way to mark a job as "filed for extension" — see ADR-012 for the deferred implementation. Until then, any SLA breach surfaced may include cases that are legally excused.
